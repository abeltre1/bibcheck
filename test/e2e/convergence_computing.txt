Convergence of Edge Computing and Deep Learning: A Test Document
X. Wang, Y. Han, V. C. M. Leung, D. Niyato, X. Yan, and X. Chen

Abstract - Ubiquitous sensors and smart devices generate massive amounts of
data at the network edge, and ever-increasing computing power is driving the
core of computation and services from the cloud to the edge. This test
document stands in for a Google-Scholar-indexed survey on the convergence of
edge computing and deep learning. Its bibliography below consists of real
citations drawn from that literature, so that bibcheck's lookup pipeline can
be exercised end to end against genuine DOIs, arXiv identifiers, and URLs.
===PAGE===
I. INTRODUCTION

As a key technology of this convergence, edge intelligence pushes model
training and inference from centralized datacenters toward the places where
data originates. Surveys of mobile edge computing chart the communication
perspective of this shift, while landmark deep learning results motivate the
demand for computation everywhere. Federated optimization demonstrates that
learning can proceed without centralizing raw data, and efficient
architectures bring inference to resource-constrained devices. Standards
bodies meanwhile define reference architectures for multi-access edge
computing. The remainder of this document exists solely to carry the
reference list on the following pages.
